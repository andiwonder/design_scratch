Hob 'N' Nob App - Proximity and Internet based social interaction tool for Graduate Students

Overview

Long hours spent in isolation are not uncommon to graduate students as they struggle to finish their college degrees. Though resources exist to help graduate students, many still struggle with academic stress, isolation and pressure to graduate on time. As a part of our design challenge, we would have to design a new system that works to counteract some of the effects that graduate students face and supports them in achieving their goals.

Hob’N’Nob was the app idea we can narrowed down to develop from a list of several idea’s that were brainstormed as a team based on the effectiveness of the solution and timeline to develop the idea. This design challenge was part of our Human Centered Computing class and the project was to help incorporate the design methodologies taught in class in solving the design challenge.

Timeline

January 2013 - April 2013

Role

Interaction Design, Visual Design, Brand Identity, User Research, Testing.

Process

As a team we began laying an outline of how we were going to approach this design challenge. As a team we decided to create an Android app for the deliverable because the team was comfortable and had experience and also that fell under one of the major smartphone OS being used by our target audience.

I began studying deeper about the problem by conducting literature reviews. Then I interviewed graduate students, graduate advisors to get more insight into the problem. Insight statements were created by analyzing the data from the interviews and literature reviews.

The scattered set of problems gathered from the interviews and insight statements were grouped into 4 main classes using affinity diagrams.

Then I used "How might we?" technique brainstormed to arrive at different feasible concepts to solve the problem. I created typical interaction scenarios for the product and tested it on users.

I created the lo-fi prototype by hand and med-fi prototype using Axure and performed various usability tests like heuristic evaluation, cognitive walkthrough, pluralistic walkthrough on these prototypes to iteratively refine the design.

We developed the app based on the final prototype design and presented during the final presentation with a working demo was also given.

Results

The application during testing had generated a huge amount of interest and testing results proved to be effective on the Graduate students in alleviating stress. Recently a similar idea was used by Facebook which would find friends nearby and people who share the same interest. With further development our product can be proven to be a very useful tool to alleviate stress in students.
